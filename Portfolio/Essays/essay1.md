# Essay 1: Evolution of My Understanding

When this course began, my understanding of telecommunications was limited to the physical infrastructure—cables, routers, and basic Wi-Fi. I viewed the field as a set of rigid protocols.

As the course progressed, my perspective shifted through three key stages:
1. **The Physical to the Logical:** Learning the OSI model helped me realize that communication is a layered abstraction. Understanding how data is encapsulated changed how I troubleshoot connectivity.
2. **The Shift to Software:** The introduction of Software Defined Networking (SDN) was a turning point. I realized that modern networks are controlled by code, not just hardware.
3. **The Intelligence Layer:** Finally, seeing how AI can manage these layers made me realize that telecommunications is moving toward "Self-Healing Networks."

This evolution has prepared me to look at networks as intelligent systems rather than just "pipes" for data.
